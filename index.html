Hi, thanks a lot for reaching to my page! If you are here, you may already know a thing or two about me, but let me introduce myself properly.

Something about me

My name is Eduardo Gambín Monserrat, though I prefer to be called just Edu since it's shorter. I am from Murcia, a city in the southeast of Spain, but these days I live in France, in a small city called Antibes, next to Nice.

I am both a mathematician and a computer science engineer. I did a special program at my university that allowed me to earn both degrees simultaneously.

It feels awkward to describe myself, but I think I am a friendly and warm person above all else. It might be because of where I am from. I tend to get along very quickly with people, which I think is a wonderful trait that I would not like to change. I also consider myself somewhat funny, often trying to crack jokes to make people laugh and feel in a good mood.

I don't think I can fit all my hobbies and passions here, but if you are interested, you can read about some of them on the About section, among many other things. I will do my best to keep it updated and interesting.

What I'm good at

This page provides a more general view of myself, for specifics you can check the CV section.

I have recently decided to aim for a PhD in Artificial Intelligence, though I am still searching for a master's degree that catches my attention. There's an itch that never leaves once you delve into the mathematics of science.

These days, I work as a C++ software developer, and I think I am good at it! Since I was very little, I have always liked tinkering with computers and machines in general. I have a genuine curiosity about how things work, regardless of the field.

I am also good at math, of course. I don't think I have a favorite subject or area, but if I had to choose one, it would be Fourier Analysis. My thesis was on this topic, and it captivated me quite a bit. It is a really interesting read, at least the introduction, which you can check out in the Projects section.

Lastly, I love physics, especially quantum physics. At some point in the future, I would like to learn more in an educational way, perhaps by pursuing a third degree.
