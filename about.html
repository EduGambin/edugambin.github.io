A love letter to Murcia

Everyone has a home and this is mine, there is so much this city has given to me

There is so much of this place in my DNA. If you asked me where is the attractiveness of it I wouldn't be able to tell right away, yet I could be talking for hours and hours about its wonders. If I had to summarize the beauty of Murcia, it would be:

> The people. Everyone is warm and friendly, people from the south are usually welcoming, but here is specially true.

> The food. Best of them are the marinera (she sailor), the pastel de carne (meat pie) and the paparajote (n.t.).

> The streets. The city breaths, and you can tell form just walking around the city center. And speaking of that, you can walk everywhere!

Hobbies

Videogames

My biggest hobby by far is playing videogames. I think this goes hand to hand with my passion for computers, and I can no longer differentiate between one and the other. My first contact with programming was through Unreal Engine 4, where the logic could be created using visual scripting through nodes. I fell in love with the idea of the computer doing exactly what I was telling it to do that I spent almost 200hrs creating small experiments of videogames. Sadly, those projects are lost forever with my old computer, but I still have the good memories I made with them.

My personal selection is Outer Wilds (which changed my life forever), the original Fallout (which taught me that limitations are just opportunities for creativity), Minecraft (this one does not need introduction), and The Beginner's Guide (which today still makes me question what is it that I want to pursue in life). I could keep going and going, but I restrain myself.

Reading is another of my passions, being fantasy my go-to genre through my life. Harry Potter, The Witcher, Mistborn, and so, so many others. Sherlock Holmes, Agatha Christie, and other mystery novels have also gotten my attention since my childhood. In fact, when I was a child, I always said that as a grown up I would like to be a detective like those in the books. To some extent, I think I have become one (not how I expected though!). These days I am more focused on reading non-fiction, with some exceptions because of a personal decision: I read every book in their original language. This proves more difficult in French, thus I read fiction in that language. In Spanish and English I am currently reading El infinito en un Junco from Irene Vallejo, and Thinking, fast and slow from Daniel Kahneman. In French, however, since I am still learning, I am (slowsly) reading La petite fille de Monsieur Linh, from Philippe Claudel. I also enjoy playing sports and doing exercise. Football is the most popular sport in Spain, so it is no surprise that it is the one I have played the most. The most that I played was in high school where I used to play in a team at regional level. These days I still try to play once or twice a week, but it sometimes proves difficult because of schedule. Swimming has also been a constant in my life, whether in the sea or in pool. I also used to go bouldering from time to time, but since I moved to France I have yet to return to that habit. Lastly, I often play the guitar. This began at university, and I have not stopped since. The best feeling in the world is to have it around and, almost unintentionally, picking it up and starting to play. There is a marvelous video that illustrates perfectly the feeling of challenge and reward one feels when playing an instrument: it produces fireworks in your brain.
